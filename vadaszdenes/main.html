Neumann János

Neumann János was a Hungarian-American mathematician and computer scientist who is considered to be one of the pioneers of the modern computer. He is best known for his work on the development of the von Neumann architecture, which is the basic framework for all modern computers. Neumann also made important contributions to the fields of game theory and nuclear physics.

Jedlik Ányos

Jedlik Ányos was a Hungarian physicist, inventor and engineer who is best known for his work on the development of the dynamo and the electric motor. He is also credited with the invention of the first self-starting dynamo and the first electric motor. Jedlik was also a pioneer in the field of electrochemistry, and he conducted extensive research on the magnetic properties of materials.

Teller Ede

Teller Ede was a Hungarian-American physicist who is best known for his work on the Manhattan Project, which developed the first atomic bomb. He also made important contributions to the fields of nuclear physics, astrophysics, and molecular physics. Teller was awarded numerous honors for his work, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

Eötvös Lóránd

Eötvös Lóránd was a Hungarian physicist and politician who is best known for his work on the Eötvös experiment, which demonstrated the equivalence of gravitational and inertial mass. He also made important contributions to the fields of thermodynamics, electromagnetic theory, and crystallography. Eötvös served as the Minister of Education and Culture in Hungary from 1919 to 1920.

Irinyi János

Irinyi János was a Hungarian chemist and inventor who is best known for his development of the noiseless match. He also made important contributions to the field of chemistry, including the discovery of the compound, picric acid. Irinyi was a member of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences and was awarded numerous honors for his work.
